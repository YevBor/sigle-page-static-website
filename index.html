Main problems in learning

Neither in school nor in college are we taught how to properly study material. We prepare for exams and memorize facts. We practice solving repetitive tasks to do better on a test, but often this does not give us real knowledge. Cramming quickly fades away and is not beneficial.

Conclusion: we also need to know how to learn, but for some reason, this is not taught in many places. What to do about this?

Specific techniques exist that can help us effectively master the material. The main thing is to understand the principles of how the brain works, how it processes information and how to use this in learning. This site is devoted to exploring modern approaches to learning and how to apply them in your life.

Effective methods for learning

Five practices from Barbra Oakley

Two types of attention

Deep knowledge arises if you alternate focused and dispersed thinking.

Recall

Remember what you have learned - this will allow you to connect scattered pieces of memory.

Interleaving

Study several skills at the same time, they enrich each other.

Questions

Listening to the teacher, come up with a good question that really worries you.

The illusion of competence

Repeat to yourself, write down, tell a friend: you only think you own the new topic.

For those who love procrastination

The story of Barbara Oakley.

From childhood, mathematics did not come easily to Barbara. She considered herself a finished humanities major, and far from the smartest. In the army, she studied Russian in order to receive a bonus, and did so successfully that she was nominated as a commander. However, in order to advance in the service, she had to pass mathematics. That's when Barbara came up with her approach to the exact sciences. It turned out that if something "doesn't come easily" to you, your hard-won knowledge is much deeper than those for whom everything is clear at first glance.
